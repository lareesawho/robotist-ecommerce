Hands-On Testing Notes

Test Environment

Tested over 14 days at Robotist Sydney — indoor controlled environment, standard office flooring, Wi-Fi 6 network. Tasks included pick-and-place, gesture mimicry via mocap, continuous walking (30 min sessions), and human-robot interaction scenarios.

Key Observations

The modular PSA actuators are the standout feature. When one joint showed wear after extended testing, we swapped the module in under 20 minutes — no return-to-factory needed. This alone makes ADAM viable for production environments where downtime matters.

Mocap training produced natural-looking gestures within 2 hours of setup. The Wi-Fi 6 link maintained sub-10ms latency for real-time control. Battery life averaged 2.5 hours under mixed workload — adequate for demos and shift-based deployment, but not all-day operation without hot-swap batteries.

The main limitation is floor surface dependency. The bipedal gait handles carpet, laminate, and polished concrete well, but thresholds above 5mm and loose cables are trip hazards. Plan your deployment space accordingly.
